ay I ask why your publisher recommended taking your older books out of circulation? I see them recommended so often between fans and then, "That sounds great, but where can I get it?" The older stuff get recommended more often because more people have read it, and I would happily pay for anything you write now, because I loved your previous stuff so much, and it whetted my appetite for more. I would have thought that other people's experience would be the same. Just curious.

As much as some people may have enjoyed them, the novels I posted on the internet were all rough first drafts that had never been edited. They’re riddled with plot holes, inconsistent characterization, continuity errors, and other problems. That’s fine for stories that are posted chapter by chapter for free on the internet as the author writes them, because that’s the accepted cultural context.

Professionally published novels come with much different expectations and a different set of standards. A lot of work goes into polishing a story for publication, with multiple people refining it over and over again to sand away all those rough edges. The investment of time and effort is much more intense, because so many people have a financial stake in the work’s success.

Now, it’s totally possible that a reader who’s been with me from the beginning may have preferred those earlier novels based on their personal tastes. For example, if you really love the bodyguard trope and the age difference kink, Close Protection may have been your favorite of my novels despite its many, many structural issues. If you dislike the “ex-lovers reuniting through angry sex” trope, you probably didn’t like Can’t Hide From Me at all.

In terms of the pure technical quality of the writing, however, Can’t Hide From Me is far superior to my earlier works. That’s in part due to the wisdom gained through years of experience, but also owes a lot to the intensive editing process a novel goes through prior to publication. Being better written doesn’t necessarily mean people will like it more - that’s down to personal tastes that are often influenced more by content than anything else. But the novel did set certain standards and expectations for the quality of my writing as I moved into the arena of professional publishing.

The earlier novels don’t measure up to those standards. There’s no way they could! And the situation my publisher and I wanted to avoid was people who hadn’t been with me for a long time comparing those works to my current and future published novels. Like it or not, I’m being viewed and judged through a different lens now.

I’m thrilled and humbled by how many people still talk about those early stories years later. I hope that a lot of people who enjoyed them back in the day got to save copies before I took them down, but I also hope people understand why I don’t want rough, unedited first drafts of my first original novels floating around the internet while I try to make a career out of writing
